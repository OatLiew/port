About Me

Cultural diversity has always been a part of me. I have been living in many different countries, primary school in Thailand, 5 years in Singapore, a year in Beijing, China and about 4 years in the States. Through these relocation, I have developed an interest to filter and absorb the positive trends of cultures. This adaptability allows me to transition smoothly into different working environments.

Through the years abroad, my experiences have molded my personal passion in the unique combination of art, architecture and engineering. At the University of Washington, I have been taking classes that lay the foundation towards my engineering career such as Digital Circuits and Systems, Computer Design and Organization, Data Structure, Microcomputer system, introduction to database, Devices and Circuits I and II and programing concept and tools. With a passion in mobile and web development, I have taken client and server side web technology courese as well as begginning android class at the UW continuing education program.

I have worked at RATAB on the Embedded System for CardioPalpation with chromaID, Prof Thomas A. And I am currently working at SEAL lab with Prof Alexander Mamishev at the University of Washington. In addition, I was part of the startup company, Foodspek, which is trying to raise the awarness of the food safety and food inspection of the restuarants in Seattle area.
